Problem

Surplus and shortage can exist in the same network.

The hub may know the big picture, but individual pantries usually do not have a shared live view of each other’s eligible inventory.

One pantry has too much

Short-dated produce, rescued food, or extra cases can sit locally.

Another pantry has need

Nearby demand may be invisible unless someone calls, texts, or asks the hub.

Time is the enemy

Perishable food loses value while people coordinate manually.

Problem addressed: match eligible surplus to shortage before food ages out.
Context: U.S. food waste is estimated at 30-40% of the food supply; food-bank networks operate at large node counts.
02 / 05
Gap Analysis

The missing layer is governed lateral movement.

Existing workflows cover donations, hub ordering, and local records. They do not create one inventory-backed marketplace for the spokes.

Food-bank ordering

Good for hub-to-agency allocation. Not designed as a shared pantry-to-pantry market.

Local inventory sheets

Useful inside one pantry. Weak for network visibility, reservations, and transfer audit.

Food rescue dispatch

Strong for donor pickup. Different problem from moving already-inventoried pantry surplus.

What GoodCo adds

  • Inventory-backed listings
  • Quantity reservation before pickup
  • Policy guardrails for restricted food
  • Audit trail for handoff and receipt
Interpretation: this is a product gap analysis, not a claim that existing food-bank systems are bad.
03 / 05
Product

Verified inventory becomes a shared market.

A pantry can only publish what it has already reviewed and confirmed. That keeps the marketplace useful instead of becoming a fake catalog.

1

Receive

Barcode, manual, photo, or voice.

2

Review

Human confirms quantity, category, and date.

3

Publish

Only eligible surplus enters the market.

4

Reserve

Claimed quantity is held for pickup.

5

Transfer

Handoff and receipt leave a record.

One marketplace Every approved spoke sees the same eligible listings.
Inventory-backed Publishing does not reduce stock. Reservation does.
Governed No payments in MVP. Restricted food can be blocked by policy.
Product boundary: GoodCo supports the hub-and-spoke model by adding lateral flow, not by replacing food-bank allocation.
04 / 05
Implementation

Built to be cheap enough to run.

The product uses free data and browser-native capture first. AI is a structured fallback, not an always-on vision pipeline.

Lookup first Open Food Facts and USDA FoodData Central before LLM parsing.
No-barcode intake Browser speech-to-text turns staff descriptions into a draft Review form.
Light AI use LLM fills structured fields only after barcode/data rules are insufficient.
Open tools Tesseract OCR, ZXing barcode scanning, Supabase/Postgres audit records.

Pilot impact metrics

  • Lots moved before expiry
  • Requests fulfilled from nearby pantries
  • Expired inventory avoided
  • Hub interventions avoided
Evidence base: lateral transshipment research finds inventory sharing can reduce cost or improve service; GoodCo still needs a pantry pilot to measure its own effect.
