Ranked by **available public/free data quality**:

1. **Theme 5: Need Is the Demand Signal**
   Best data fit. Census ACS, CalFresh, CHIS, Food Access Atlas, Healthy Places Index, CalEnviroScreen, FoodNow/SF-Marin locators. This is the strongest theme if you want credible neighborhood-level demand/access modeling.

2. **Theme 1: See It Coming**
   Strong external data for crop supply, produce prices, market movement, weather, traffic, grants, and funding. Weakness: donor reliability and actual inbound history still require internal food-bank data.

3. **Theme 4: Equity With a Truck Attached**
   Strong for routing, traffic, weather, EV charging, grid prices, and neighborhood access constraints. Weakness: actual agency cancellations, product urgency, driver service time, and fleet status are internal.

4. **Theme 7: A Smarter Movement**
   Strong for SNAP/economic impact modeling, medium for surplus/shortage signals, weak for true cross-food-bank ops benchmarking. The policy-impact idea is very buildable; the anonymized network benchmark idea needs private data.

5. **Theme 3: Production Is Manufacturing**
   Medium. You can use demand, volunteer, food waste, and food insecurity datasets, but the real production problems need internal line data: volunteer attendance, kit demand, station setup, yield, waste, and task timing.

6. **Theme 6: Every Team Member an Analyst**
   Medium-to-weak as a data product. Good for a demo that queries public/internal data in plain English, but the strongest value depends on connecting internal WMS/TMS/CRM/SOP data. Without that, it risks becoming a generic chatbot.

7. **Theme 2: The Warehouse That Explains Itself**
   Weakest public-data fit. Almost every valuable problem needs internal warehouse data: bin maps, scanner events, pick history, inventory adjustments, cycle counts, forklift maintenance, dock activity, and product movement velocity.

Best build direction from a data standpoint: **Theme 5**, with some pieces from **Theme 1** and **Theme 4**. That gives you a credible product using real public data without pretending you have food-bank internal systems.
